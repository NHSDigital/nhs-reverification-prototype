Add or correct the contact details on your NHS record

It's important your NHS record has your correct contact details.

This is because your GP surgery and other NHS services use them to contact you about screening, vaccinations or appointments.

This service lets you update or correct a missing or invalid mobile number or email address on your NHS record.

The contact details on your NHS record may be different from the details your GP surgery has for you. It's important both are correct and up to date.

Who can use this service

If you have been contacted by the NHS

If the NHS has sent you a text or email to tell you the mobile number or email address on your NHS record is missing, or is in an incorrect format, you can use this service to update it.

For example, if your NHS record does not have all 11 digits of your mobile number, you can fix it with this service.

If you have not been contacted by the NHS

If you have an NHS account or the NHS App, you can use them to:

  • add a missing mobile number or email
  • fix an issue with a mobile number or email

Log in to your NHS account or use the NHS App.

If you do not have an NHS account or the NHS App or if you want to change other details, contact your GP surgery.

Find a GP

Before you start

You will need access to your mobile phone or email account. This is so the service can send you security codes.

What happens next

To verify it's you, a security code will be sent to the mobile number or email address on your NHS record.

Once confirmed, a different security code will be sent to the contact details you're adding or updating, to check it's working correctly.
